01 Find Your Why Steps 1-3 · Watch + worksheet

Most people think podcasting starts with gear. It starts with deciding why you're actually doing this. Everything else gets easier after that.

▶ Watch: Surface reason vs honest reason
⬇ Download: Find Your Why worksheet
01
Know why you're really doing this
"I want to grow a podcast" is fine, but it's usually not the real reason. Write two answers: the surface reason, and the honest one. Then finish: "I'm creating this podcast because…" My honest reason: I wanted better conversations than the ones I was seeing online, not just a bigger audience.
02
Decide what you want people to leave with
Every episode leaves something behind: a thought, a feeling, a question. Ask yourself what you want to stay with someone after they finish listening. It doesn't need to be deep, but it should be intentional.
03
Be clear about what your podcast actually is
You don't need a tiny niche, but people should understand what they're stepping into. If someone reads your description and instantly knows whether it's for them, you're on the right track.
02 Name & Identity Steps 4-6
04
Pick a name you'll still like later
Say it out loud: "Welcome to…" Does it feel natural? If not, change it now, it's much easier than changing it later.
05
I used to think...
Write one sentence that keeps you grounded
This is your anchor, it shows up in your bio, captions, and intros. I used to think I needed a clever tagline. I landed on "Raw conversations, meet your edge" instead, one honest sentence that reminds me not to overthink it, not to perform, just to have real conversations.
06
Pay attention to other podcasts, but don't copy them
Look for what's missing. Where do conversations stay safe? Where do you switch off as a listener? Your advantage is usually just being willing to go a bit further in honesty.
03 Format & Commitment Steps 7-9 · Watch

Pick something simple and repeatable. Consistency beats complexity every time.

▶ Watch: My actual content calendar
07
Decide how your episodes will actually look
One guest per episode works. Remote or in person both work. Video or audio both work. Stick with one format for your first 10 episodes, that's what matters more than which format you pick.
08
Make a commitment you can actually keep
Most people burn out trying to post too much too soon. Start with 1 episode per week and 3-5 short clips. Consistency beats intensity.
09
I nearly made this mistake
Start with people you already know
Don't wait for big guests. I nearly delayed my launch waiting for someone "impressive" to say yes first. My first episodes ended up being with people I already had real conversations with, and that's what made them work. Nobody cares how big your guest is, they care if the conversation is worth listening to.
04 Build Your Setup Steps 10-18 · Watch + 3 downloads

This is where most people get stuck, trying to build the perfect setup before they've recorded anything. You get better by recording, not by researching gear.

▶ Watch: My real folders, Spotify dashboard & Auphonic settings
⬇ Download: Guest tracker template ⬇ Download: Folder structure starter ⬇ Download: Auphonic settings cheat sheet
10
Start with good enough
You don't need a studio. A decent microphone, good lighting, and a quiet space is enough. Nobody ever asked what camera I used, they cared about the conversation.
11
I changed my mind about this
Choose how you're recording
Remote gives you access to anyone. In person creates a different energy. I assumed I needed a proper studio setup before recording. I changed my mind fast, some of my favourite episodes were outside in a garden with natural light. It doesn't need to be perfect to be good.
12
Set up your podcast
Use Spotify for Podcasters for publishing audio and video, then upload every episode to YouTube in 4K for search and long-term discovery. Before launch, make sure your artwork, description, and profile are complete, then stop touching it and focus on conversations.
13
Create a simple Instagram
Use your podcast name. Keep it clean: name, bio with your mission sentence, simple profile image, one link to your podcast. That's it.
14
Keep your artwork simple
Most people will see it as a tiny thumbnail. Clear text, strong contrast, easy to recognise. Complicated artwork doesn't work at that size.
15
I used to think...
Build an editing workflow you can stick to
I used to think better editing meant more software and more steps. It didn't, it just meant more time lost. I settled on Premiere Pro and Auphonic, with Adaptive Leveler (Default Leveler, 100% strength), Voice AutoEQ filtering, Loudness Normalisation at -16 LUFS, and Speech Isolation noise reduction all switched on, and stopped touching the rest. Full settings are in the download below. Don't overthink this part, you'll improve as you go.
16
Get organised early
Set up folders before you record anything: Raw, Episodes, Exports, Clips. Get a 1TB SSD, it saves headaches later.
17
Keep track of your guests
A simple spreadsheet: name, contact, status, follow-up, episode ideas. Don't overcomplicate it, just don't rely on memory.
18
I changed my mind about this
Record before you launch
I originally planned to announce the podcast and record as I went. I changed my mind and recorded 3 episodes before telling anyone about it. That gave me breathing room. Aim for 3 to 5 episodes before launch, it changes everything.
05 Recording Mindset Steps 19-21 · Watch

This is the part that actually matters. People don't come back for gear, they come back for conversations that felt real.

▶ Watch: The Wim Hof guided breathing session I use
19
Something I've never skipped
Slow down before you record
People feel your energy. From day one, my guest and I do 11 minutes of guided Wim Hof breathing together before every single recording. It's not something I made, just one I use every time. It stops being an interview and becomes a conversation. Find whatever helps you get into that state.
20
Make your guest feel safe
People don't open up because of questions, they open up because they feel comfortable. Tell them what to expect, talk before recording, don't pressure answers, let silence happen.
21
Prepare your questions, then forget them
Prepare questions, then stop using them once you start. They're direction, not a script. Curiosity beats clever questions every time.
06 The Conversation Itself Steps 22-26 · Watch
▶ Watch: Listening for the next question (real example)
22
Know enough about your guest before you start
I don't go in with a written list of follow-ups, I just make sure I actually know who I'm talking to beforehand. That research gives the conversation somewhere to go without it feeling scripted, and it's where my real follow-ups come from: "Can you say more about that?" or "What was going through your mind?"
23
Don't force depth
Not every conversation needs to go deep. Some people go there naturally, some don't, both are fine. Read the room, let it happen, don't force it.
24
Be present, not perfect
Silence is not a problem, it's part of the conversation. Some of the best moments happen when you say nothing and just let someone think.
25
Notice the moments that matter
You'll feel it when something lands: a pause, a shift in tone, a moment that feels real. Those are your clips. Those are what people remember.
26
Reflect for two minutes after
After every recording, pause and ask: what felt real, when did it open up, what did I learn. That habit compounds over time.
07 Launch Steps 27-28 · Download
⬇ Download: Launch-day checklist
27
Where the real growth begins
Get your first 3 episodes recorded and out there
Don't wait for a perfect tenth episode plan. Get 3 to 5 episodes recorded, then launch. I shared mine with friends first before going fully public, and that early support mattered more than I expected. But the real shift happens the moment people can actually press play. Stop preparing and start publishing.
28
Get your basics ready
Before launch: description, clips, trailer, links. Then stop tweaking and start recording.
08 Sustain & Grow Steps 29-35 · Watch

Starting is easy. Continuing is where it gets real.

▶ Watch: Protecting your energy
29
Share moments that matter
Don't chase viral clips. Ask: would this help someone, would I send this to a friend. That's enough.
30
Build a simple system
After each episode: edit, export, clip, post. Keep it repeatable. Less thinking, more doing.
31
Look after your first listeners
Reply to comments. Say thanks. Talk to people. Your first listeners matter more than your thousandth.
32
Track what actually matters
Not just views. Look at watch time, repeat listeners, messages, shares. That tells you if it's working.
33
I got this wrong
Protect your energy
If you're drained, your conversations suffer. I got this wrong early on, recording back to back with no recovery time. I learned the hard way. Rest is part of the process.
34
Let it evolve
Your podcast will change as you do. Every 10-20 episodes, ask: what feels good, what doesn't, what do I want more of. Don't force consistency at the cost of authenticity.
35
Know when to expand
If something grows naturally from your podcast, explore it: community, workshops, partnerships. But don't rush it, let it come from trust. Honestly, I'm still figuring this part out myself, so take this one as "in progress" rather than a finished answer.
