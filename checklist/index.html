Launch Checklist

Phase 1 Decide & Define Steps 1–8 · Before you buy anything
01
Get brutally clear on WHY you're doing this
Not "to grow an audience." Go deeper. What do you actually want to say? Who needs to hear it? Write it in one sentence. If you can't, keep thinking. the clarity comes before the camera.
02
Decide on your show concept. niche or no niche
You don't need a tight niche. Raw, honest conversations about mindset, identity and growth. broad enough for anyone, specific enough for the right listener. Ask: who would choose this show during their limited attention window?
03
Name the show
Edge Within came from the idea that the growth you're looking for lives inside you. Keep it short. Make it mean something. Test it out loud. does it feel right when you say "Welcome to [name]"?
04
Write your show's one-line mission statement
"Real, raw, honest conversations. Meet your edge within." One line. Anyone who hears it instantly gets it. This becomes your bio, your caption opener, your show intro.
05
Research 3–5 shows in your space
Not to copy them to find the gap. What's working? What feels scripted or surface-level? Your unique angle lives in what others aren't willing to do.
06
Define your episode format
Long-form interview, 45–90 min, unscripted, one guest. No intro music. straight into the conversation. Decide: interview, solo, or both? Remote or in-person? Pick one and stick to it for the first 10 episodes.
07
Set your posting commitment and write it down
1 full episode per week + 3–5 short clips per week on Instagram. Don't overcommit. Consistency beats volume every time. A show posting once a week for a year beats daily posting for a month then going quiet.
08
Mat did this
List your first 5 potential guests
Start with friends and people you already know. No shame in this. your first 3 episodes don't need to be famous people. They need to be real conversations. Trust over credentials every time.
Phase 2 Set Up Steps 9–16 · Equipment, accounts, and systems
09
Choose your recording setup
A full studio setup from day one is ideal. dedicated mics, lighting, camera. But an iPhone in good light still beats waiting 6 months for gear. The conversation is what matters. Start with what you have.
10
Mat did this
Find your recording location
Mat recorded his first episodes in his back garden under trees for shade. Not a studio. Not a soundproofed room. A quiet outdoor spot with natural light and no echo. Think: ambient sound, no echo, good light. Surprisingly, outdoor works well.
11
Choose your hosting platform and set it up
Your hosting platform distributes your audio to Spotify, Apple Podcasts and more. Options: Buzzsprout, Podbean, Anchor (free). Set up your account and upload your show art. You don't need an episode ready. just have it live.
12
Create the show's Instagram account
Keep it clean. showname + podcast if needed. Bio = your mission statement. Link in bio. Profile photo = show logo, not a personal photo. You're building a brand.
13
Design your show logo and cover art
Use Canva. Bold text, your face, clear branding. It needs to be legible at thumbnail size. The biggest mistake is over-designing. bold and simple wins every time.
14
Set up your editing software
Premiere Pro is the industry standard and worth learning. Free alternatives: DaVinci Resolve (video), Audacity (audio only). Don't let software be the reason you don't start. CapCut on your phone is enough for first clips.
15
Set up external storage for your files
Raw video files are huge. Get at minimum a 1TB external hard drive or cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox). You will run out of laptop space faster than you think. sort this before episode one.
16
Mat did this
Build a content bank before you launch
Mat recorded 3 episodes before posting a single thing. This is the move. Don't launch with one episode. you look like everyone else who started and stopped. Have 3–5 episodes ready so you can post consistently from day one and not scramble after every recording.
Phase 3 Record & Launch Steps 17–24 · Get your first episodes done
17
Book your first 3 guests
Keep it simple: "I'm launching a podcast about [topic], I think you'd be a great first guest. 45 mins, in person, I'll handle everything. Are you in?" People say yes more than you think.
18
Prepare for each conversation. not a script, a direction
5–10 questions prepared, but be willing to throw them out the moment the conversation goes somewhere real. Research your guest, find what they're passionate about, then let curiosity lead. The best moments are never planned.
19
Mat did this
Do a pre-recording ritual to get in state
Mat uses breathwork with his guest before recording. Even 2 minutes of intentional breathing changes the energy in the room. You're asking someone to be vulnerable on camera. help them arrive. The quality of your conversations depends on the state both people are in.
20
Mat did this
Record your first 3 episodes
Mat's first location: his back garden under trees. Natural shade, no echo, good natural light. Don't wait for a perfect setup. The first episode is never your best. it's just proof you started. Record it, learn from it, get to episode two.
21
Edit your long-form episode
Keep the long-form for your hosting platform and YouTube. Cut dead air, ums that break flow, and anything that loses energy. But don't over-polish. real conversations have natural pauses. That's what makes them human.
22
Pull 3–5 short clips for Instagram Reels
This is where the growth comes from. One 60-min episode = 5–8 clips of 30–90 seconds each. Look for strong opinions, surprising insights, or emotional turns. The clip should make someone stop scrolling and think "I need to hear more."
23
Write captions that extend the conversation
Don't describe the clip. extend it. Open with a hook, expand on the idea in 2–3 lines, tag the guest, add a CTA. The caption is a second piece of content.
24
Mat did this
Launch. post your first 3 episodes at once
Mat dropped 3 episodes on January 20, 2026. Not one. Three. It signals to anyone who finds you that this is a real show with depth. not a trial. It also gives the algorithm more to work with. Launch day should feel like an arrival, not a test.
Phase 4 Grow Steps 25–30 · What happens after launch day
25
Post consistently. even when it feels pointless
Post 3–5 times per week and don't stop. At 444 followers it feels like shouting into nothing. At 2,684 it starts compounding. At 9,468 it feels like momentum. Push through the quiet phase. everyone who built something had to.
26
Tag every guest and collaborate on the post
Tag every guest and encourage them to share. Even a guest with 2,000 followers sharing your clip puts you in front of people who already trust them. Free reach. Never skip this.
27
Start cold outreach for guests beyond your network
DM someone you admire. Lead with genuine praise, mention something specific about their work, then pitch the show concisely. Don't ask for a favour. offer them a platform. After 5–8 episodes your show is your proof.
28
Review every episode. what worked, what didn't
Review clip performance weekly. Which clips got reach? Which fell flat? What topics resonated? The algorithm gives you data constantly. use it. Your show at month 6 should look different to month 1.
29
Build your media kit once you have traction
Once you have real numbers. even modest ones. build a one-page media kit. 10,700 followers and 2.3M monthly views in 160 days is a story sponsors listen to. You don't need 100K to start the conversation.
30
Don't stop. The show compounds.
444 followers at day 48. 10,700 at day 160. The first 50 days felt slow. The next 110 were exponential. Every episode makes the next guest easier to book, the next viewer easier to convert. The work compounds. Keep going.
