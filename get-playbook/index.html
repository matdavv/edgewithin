The Real Problem

You don't need another reason to start.
You need a system.

Maybe you've been sitting on the idea for months. Maybe you've launched and it already feels harder than it should.

Guests are slow to say yes. Episodes pile up unedited. Growth feels random.

None of that means you're bad at this. It usually just means nobody handed you the order to do things in. Or the actual tools to do it with.

That's what this playbook is. The order. Plus the templates and videos to back it up.

Why Most Podcasts Never Get Off The Ground

You don't need more motivation.
You need the order.

🔁

You're posting when you feel like it

Growth isn't a streak of good days, it's a system that runs whether you feel inspired or not.

🛠️

You're polishing the wrong things

Better mic. Better intro. Better thumbnail. None of it matters if episode three never gets recorded because episode one took six weeks to plan.

📉

You're chasing downloads, not moments

A clip that makes someone stop scrolling does more for a new show than a thousand passive listens ever will.

⏳

You're waiting until you're "ready"

I started with zero audience, zero guests, and no plan beyond the next 7 days. The order is what got me to over 2M views, not permission, not equipment, not a bigger following.

You Don't Need a Big Audience

How I booked great guests
with 0 followers.

I had 0 followers when I booked my first guest. Here's what actually moved the needle.

🎯

Give people a reason, not a pitch

Nobody says yes to "I have a podcast." They say yes to a specific question they've never been asked, or a story they want to tell.

🔂

Small and consistent beats big and occasional

One guest a week, every week, builds a pipeline faster than chasing one "dream guest" who never replies.

🚪

Every guest is a door to the next one

A good conversation gets shared. A shared conversation gets noticed by someone else worth talking to.

💬

The show doesn't need to be big, it needs to be real

People can tell the difference between a host performing and a host actually curious, and they say yes to the second one.
